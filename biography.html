Dr. Gerald Niznick
History

Dr. Gerald A. Niznick is the founder and President of Core-Vent Dental Implant Company from 1982 to 2001 (1997-2001 dba. Paragon Implant Company). Barron's Magazine Article on Dental Implant Industry Feb. 15, 2005 referred to Dr. Niznick as "a prosthodontist and entrepreneur who is considered by many as the godfather of American implant dentistry." On January 7th, 2001, Dr. Niznick sold Core-Vent/Paragon to Sulzer Medica which was subsequently acquired by Zimmer Dental. He retained ownership of the 40,000 square foot Calabasas California factory designed and built specifically for the manufacture of dental implants in 1994. Dr. Niznick formed Implant Direct LLC in early 2004, to develop a new dental implant product line which he launched in November 2006. On December 31, 2010, he sold 75% of that company to Danaher/Sybron forming a new Joint Venture which he remained as president through 2013 and in January 2014, Dr. Niznick sold his remaining interest in Implant Direct Joint Venture to Danaher/Sybron but continued as a consultant through March 2017. From 1982-2017, a 35 year span, Dr. Niznick was issued 33 U.S. Patents on dental implant related products. Dr. Niznick graduated from the University of Manitoba Dental School in 1966, spent a year in postgraduate prosthodontics at USC (1966-67) and then earned a Masters degree in Prosthodontics at Indiana University (1967-69). Throughout the 1980's and 90's he personally trained over 10,000 dentists worldwide on the placement and restoration of dental implants using lectures and live surgical demonstrations. Dr. Niznick's significant contributions to the dental implant field has been recognized by academic institutions and dental implant organizations. He has received Honorary Doctorate degrees from the University of Manitoba and from Tel Aviv University, as well as the prestigious Isaah Lew Research award from the American Academy of Implant Dentistry. The United States Department of Veterans Affairs issued a Commendation to Dr. Niznick for conceiving and funding the largest dental implant study worldwide at 32 VA centers that included 900 patients receiving over 2800 of Dr. Niznickâ€™s implants. Special Issues of the Journal of Periodontology and the Journal of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery were dedicated to publishing the results of this monumental prospective, multi-center clinical study, which added significantly to dental implant knowledge.
